ACI
The Agresti-Coull interval. We use the ACI to generate a single number that takes into both how successful a hero has been and the number of times it has been played.

Higher is better.

Technical Details
We calculate the lower bound of a 95% confidence interval, then multiply by a constant for a prettier number. For more discussion, see this stackoverflow question.
Examples
For example, a win ACI of 35.0 means that, based on your games played on that hero, the lower bound of the 95% confidence interval for your win rate for that hero is 35.
On the difference between ACI and hero points
Sorting by your win ACI will likely do a good job of showing you the hero you're most likely to win with based on your data so far. In contrast, hero points are more about how you perform on that hero, relative to the people you play with and against. That means you may do better than most people on a hero and thus garner a high hero score, but if the hero sucks then you'll still lose quite a bit (looking at you, Meepo). Keep this in mind when comparing hero points and win ACI.
